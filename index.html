Amina's Story

Amina, 16

Student & Tech Learner

Joined HopeLink in 2024

When Amina arrived at Mahama Refugee Camp, she had lost everything - her home, her school, and her friends. For months, she felt hopeless, wondering if she would ever have a future.

"I thought my education was over. I thought I would never learn again or have opportunities like other girls my age."

Then she discovered HopeLink's Youth Empowerment program. Through our digital skills hub, Amina learned computer basics, coding, and digital literacy. But more importantly, she found confidence and purpose.

"Through HopeLink, I gained computer skills and confidence to help others. Now I teach younger children basic computer skills. I feel like I'm contributing to my community."

Amina is now one of our star students, helping to teach younger children in the camp. She dreams of becoming a software developer and creating technology solutions for refugee communities.

Jean's Story

Jean, 18

Youth Leader

HopeLink Youth Ambassador

Jean arrived at Mahama Camp as a quiet, reserved teenager. Like many youth in the camp, he struggled to find his voice and purpose in the challenging environment.

"I felt invisible. No one asked what I thought or what I wanted for my future. HopeLink changed that completely."

Through HopeLink's youth leadership program, Jean discovered his passion for community organizing. He learned communication skills, project management, and how to advocate for his community's needs.

"HopeLink gave me a platform to make my voice heard and create positive change. We started a youth council that now advises camp management on issues affecting young people."

Today, Jean leads the Mahama Youth Council, representing over 1,000 young people in the camp. He's helped organize clean-up campaigns, started a peer tutoring program, and advocated for better educational facilities.

Marie's Story

Marie, 32

Community Mother

Mental Wellness Program Participant

Marie arrived at Mahama Camp with three young children after fleeing violence in her home country. The trauma of displacement left her struggling with depression and anxiety, making it difficult to care for her family.

"After losing everything, I didn't think I could go on. The weight was too heavy. HopeLink gave me hope again and helped me heal."

Marie joined HopeLink's Mental Wellness program, where she found a supportive community and professional counseling. Through group therapy sessions and individual counseling, she learned coping strategies and began to heal.

"The support groups became my family. We shared our stories, our pain, and our hope. For the first time, I didn't feel alone in my struggle."

Today, Marie leads a support group for other mothers in the camp. She uses her experience to help others navigate the challenges of displacement and trauma recovery. She also volunteers at the HopeLink community kitchen, cooking meals for families in need.
