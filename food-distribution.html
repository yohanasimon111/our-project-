Food Security in Mahama Refugee Camp

Comprehensive food distribution and nutrition programs for 60,000+ refugees

Overview

Mahama Refugee Camp faces significant food security challenges with over 60,000 residents relying on humanitarian assistance. Our comprehensive food distribution system ensures regular access to nutritious food while specialized nutrition programs target the most vulnerable groups including children under 5, pregnant and lactating women, elderly, and individuals with chronic illnesses. Through strategic partnerships and community-based approaches, we're working to eliminate malnutrition and build food resilience.

Distribution Schedule & Process

  • Bi-weekly distributions on 1st and 15th of each month
  • Digital registration and e-voucher system for efficiency
  • Priority access for vulnerable groups and persons with disabilities
  • Community volunteers assist with distribution logistics

Standard Food Basket

Maize flour (12kg per person/month)
Beans (3kg per person/month)
Cooking oil (1L per person/month)
Corn Soy Blend (2kg per child/month)

Neighborhood Distribution Points

Decentralized access points for community convenience

Location Strategy

  • Strategic placement within 1km of all residential zones
  • Mobile distribution for remote or inaccessible areas
  • Extended hours during distribution days (6AM-6PM)
  • Separate queues for vulnerable groups to reduce waiting time
